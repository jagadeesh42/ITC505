Advancing Cybersecurity through Federated Learning

Current Approaches and Future Directions

Author: Jagadeesh Dhulipudi

Abstract

Federated Learning (FL) has emerged as a transformative paradigm in machine learning, allowing multiple parties to collaboratively train models without sharing raw data. This paper explores its applications in cybersecurity, focusing on architectures, attack resilience, and optimization strategies.

1. Introduction

With the growth of cyber threats, the need for privacy-preserving and collaborative security mechanisms has intensified. FL provides a framework to train robust intrusion detection models while ensuring sensitive organizational data remains local.

2. Federated Learning Architectures in Cybersecurity

Different FL architectures such as cross-device and cross-silo models have been applied to distributed network security. These approaches enable collaborative defense mechanisms across organizations.

3. Attacks and Defenses in FL

FL introduces new vulnerabilities, including model poisoning and inference attacks. Defensive measures such as differential privacy, secure aggregation, and anomaly detection are critical to safeguard the system.

4. Optimization and Personalization

Personalized FL strategies ensure that models adapt to heterogeneous data distributions. Techniques like clustered FL and transfer learning improve applicability in real-world cybersecurity.

5. Conclusion

FL holds significant promise for advancing cybersecurity by enabling secure collaboration. Future research should explore hybrid approaches combining blockchain, edge computing, and federated reinforcement learning.
